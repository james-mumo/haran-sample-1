About Us

Horn of Africa Aid and Rehabilitation Action Network (HAARAN)

is an independent organization focused on promoting an inclusive society for persons with disabilities and vulnerable individuals. Founded in 2008 by Abdi Noor Matan, HAARAN addresses the challenges faced by Somali immigrants and expands its reach to Kenya and Somalia, empowering communities through capacity-building. The organization advocates for disability rights as human rights, operating on a community-based inclusive development approach to ensure equality, participation, and resilience for all individuals.

HAARAN envisions a society where the vulnerable persons have the right to an active, healthy and dignified life.

HAARAN THEMATIC AREAS

Inclusive Education and Skills Development.
Education For African Children
Read More

Inclusive education and skills development are essential in empowering African children to reach their full potential, ensuring they have access to quality learning opportunities regardless of their background or abilities. By fostering an environment where every child is supported, we can bridge educational gaps and equip future generations with the skills necessary to thrive in a globalized world.

Promoting sustainable opportunities for inclusive growth and resilience.
Economic Empowerment and Livelihoods
Read More

By promoting sustainable opportunities, individuals and marginalized groups, including persons with disabilities, can gain access to skills training, entrepreneurship support, and income-generating activities. These efforts not only improve financial independence but also contribute to breaking cycles of poverty and inequality, enabling communities to thrive in the face of economic challenges.

Disability Rights & Protection in Higher Education
Governance and Rights
Read More

Disability rights and protection in higher education ensure that students with disabilities have equal access to educational opportunities, accommodations, and support services. Promoting inclusive governance and rights within academic institutions creates a more equitable environment where all individuals, regardless of ability, can thrive and contribute to the academic community.

KEY ACHIEVEMENTS
  • Participated in a global United Nations meeting in New York, South Africa, Kenya, and Ethiopia on the development of UNCRPD – toolkit for African nations.
  • HAARAN is on the frontline promoting inclusive learning in Daadab Refugee Camp.
  • Submitted memoranda on several disability policies in Somalia to the Ministry of Education through our partners.
  • Organized a youth with disabilities knowledge transfer summit in collaboration with EduCab and Garissa University using the smart library approach.
  • Received Gustavus Adolphus College 2018 Community Partner Presidents' Award for Civic Engagement in the United States.
  • Distributed assistive devices to persons with disabilities in the Horn of Africa.
